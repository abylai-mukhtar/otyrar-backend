Director's Blog

Letters and reflections from the director

Yerlan Bekzhanov
Director of the Otrar State Museum-Reserve

Director of the Otrar State Archaeological Museum-Reserve and Candidate of Historical Sciences, with over 15 years of service at the museum.

Specialises in the history and culture of Silk Road states in Central Asia. Author of more than 50 scientific articles and monographs.

Currently implementing a programme to develop the museum, expand international partnerships, and improve tourist infrastructure.

Q & A

What is the museum's main mission?
The museum primary mission is to preserve, study, and promote the historical and cultural heritage of the Great Silk Road and the Otrar region. Passing this rich legacy on to future generations is our responsibility.
Are new projects planned?
In 2024–2025, the museum will implement several major projects: new interactive exhibition halls will open, and an online tour platform will be launched.
What advice do you have for visitors?
I recommend arriving early in the morning to avoid queues. Take a guided tour — each artefact has its own story to tell.
What is the museum international significance?
Otrar is one of the leading centres for the study of medieval Central Asian history. We aim to have the settlement included in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

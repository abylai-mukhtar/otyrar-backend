About the Museum

Otrar State Archaeological Museum-Reserve

Museum History

1979
The Otrar State Historical and Cultural Museum-Reserve was founded by decision of the Ministry of Culture of the Kazakh SSR.
1990-2000
Expansion period: the museum's territory was enlarged to 11,000 hectares and new exhibition halls were opened.
2001-2010
International scientific cooperation: joint expeditions with scholars from Japan, Germany, and France.
2011-2020
Modernization: all museum exhibitions were updated and modern digital equipment was installed.
2021–present
Tourism development: new routes, online tours, and active international partnerships.

The Otrar State Archaeological Museum-Reserve is one of Kazakhstan's largest historical and cultural institutions, holding over 24,825 artifacts and 76 historical monuments across 11,000 hectares.

Museum Structure

🏛️ Exhibition Department

Historical and cultural exhibitions, interactive halls, and guided tour programs

🔬 Research Department

Archaeological research, scientific publications, and international cooperation

📦 Collection Department

Storage, cataloguing, and digitisation of 24,825 artefacts

🛡️ Heritage Protection

Monitoring and restoration of 76 historical and cultural monuments

📚 Library

Collection of books on history, archaeology, and regional studies

🎓 Education Department

School programmes, workshops, and educational lectures

Visitor Rules

  • Entry by ticket purchased at the box office or online.
  • Do not touch the exhibits.
  • Please maintain silence in the exhibition halls.
  • Food and drink only in designated areas.
  • Photography permitted (no flash). Video recording requires prior approval.
  • Children under 7 must be accompanied by an adult at all times.
  • Opening hours: Monday–Sunday 9:00–18:00.

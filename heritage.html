Historical Heritage

Historical monuments of the Otrar region

Republic-level monument

Otrar Settlement

Otrar is the site of a medieval city that served as an important node on the Great Silk Road. The city emerged in the 1st century BC and flourished throughout the Middle Ages.

During the Mongol invasion of 1219, Otrar heroically withstood a five-month siege. The great scholar Abu Nasr al-Farabi was born here.

The Otrar settlement has been nominated for inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Archaeological excavations continue to this day.

12th century

Arystanbab Mausoleum

The Arystanbab Mausoleum is one of the most important religious sites in southern Kazakhstan, historically serving as a spiritual centre for Sufi masters and disciples.

The mausoleum was built in the 12th–14th centuries and comprises a mosque, mausoleum, and madrasa. Visiting Arystanbab is considered a prerequisite for pilgrimage to the Khoja Ahmed Yasawi Mausoleum.

Thousands of pilgrims and tourists visit the Arystanbab Mausoleum every year.

1st century BC – 19th century

Ancient City of Otrar

The cultural layers of Ancient Otrar span history from the 1st century BC to the 19th century.

Ceramics, coins, and jewellery found during excavations vividly illustrate the life of medieval urban dwellers.

Ancient Otrar was one of the most important trading centres along the Great Silk Road.

Medieval

Oksyz Settlement

Oksyz is a medieval settlement located in the heart of the Otrar oasis.

Excavations have made it possible to study urban culture from the 6th to 12th centuries.

Research at this site has played an important role in documenting local history.
